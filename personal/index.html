My primary hobby (I hope, my profession in the future as well) is Software Engineering. When I was 8, I started to learn Java, though several years later I switched to a RAD-studio DevelNext and used to work a lot in JPHP, by doing small educational projects. I've tried Python and JavaScript, but I don't really like them. Nowadays, most of the time I work in С++. I've been learning how programming languages work in general and designing my own. At school, I used to write a Wiki (now, a sort of documentation) about Java for my classmates, when we learned it.
In 2020 I found out what Linux is, and did distro-hopping to find the most fitting distro out-of-the-box — for me it was Ubuntu Budgie. I've gotten to understand various Linux-related terms, components, etc. I used to carry to my school a live USB with that distro on it. I still actively use it at home since the beginning of 2022, however, I also work with the stripped Debian (debootstrap). I'm going to switch to KDE Plasma in some distant future.
Linux isn't scary, it's just powerful. That's like a myriad of free (libre and gratis), customizable, simple, and advanced OSs for everyone. Android is also based on Linux ;)
When I have spare time, I learn how human languages work as well; that includes phonetics (sounds), morphology (words), syntax (sentences), and other sciences. Believe me or not, that's not that easy: one should understand all those tenses, aspects, moods, affixes, and so on — and that's only verbs. I've been creating my own conlang, but it doesn't have any words yet and is unnamed.
Some day, in October or November of 2017 my friend (and a former classmate) brought to school some book by Erin Hunter (I don't exactly remember which one). I asked him, what was it, and then skimmed through random words. I concluded that it was either weird or childish, although the next day I became so curious to bring a copy of one of the "WarriorS" books (in Russian) and started reading it together with him "just for fun". After I had finished the Prologue and two chapters, I actually became a fan. From that day forward, in a couple of weeks I had already finished a whole arc (6 books). At the moment, I've read 71 books (9 of them are mangas though). I participate in the Warrior Cats fandom to this day.
Well, there are 1378 characters, dead or alive x)

It's a massive and friendly, diverse and unique fandom, which I consider myself a part of, even though I don't actively do anything. And yes, I have a fursona, which is shown at the top of this page where a photo should have been. If don't know who furries are and what they do, you may be interested in checking out my classmate's and my school web-development project, a website about furries: site [uk].

yaWeather

A simple weather software that works with over 300,000 cities over the world, shows the forecast for every 3 hours 5 days ahead. You can check the temperature, atmospheric pressure, humidity, wind speed and direction, and a big detailed description of the current weather. Imperial, metric, and SI units are supported.
